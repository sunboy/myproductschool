Chapter 2 · Define before you solve
F — Frame
"A precise problem statement is worth more than ten solutions. Engineers default to solutions — great PMs default to questions."

The framing trap

Most engineers presented with a product problem immediately begin generating solutions. This is deeply wired — it's what makes engineers effective at their jobs. But in product thinking, jumping to solutions before framing the problem is the single most common failure mode.

The FLOW principle: You cannot optimize a solution to the wrong problem. Framing comes first — always.

What good framing looks like

Good framing answers three questions before any solution is considered: Who has the problem? What are they actually trying to do? And what does success look like for them?

Notice these are not technical questions. They are human questions. The best product engineers learn to hold two mental models simultaneously: the technical and the human.
