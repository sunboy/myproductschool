## Practice Tips Compilation

### Record Yourself
- Answer questions on your phone/computer
- Review for clarity, pace, and structure
- Identify where you ramble or jump ahead

### Peer Review
- Have someone ask the question
- Let them interrupt with follow-ups
- Get feedback on thinking clarity

### Time Yourself
- Aim for 8-12 minutes per question in interview
- Practice with timer for discipline
- Build speed while maintaining quality

### Focus on Process, Not Answer
- Interviewers want to see HOW you think
- There's rarely a "right" answer
- Show your reasoning step-by-step

### Build Your Favorite Product Answer
- This question appears in 90% of interviews
- Spend time finding a product you genuinely love
- Practice explaining why 3 different ways
- Have 3 improvements ready to discuss

## Evaluation Checklist

### Questions to Ask Yourself After Practice:

**Communication** (Clarity)
- Did I explain each step clearly?
- Would the interviewer follow my logic?
- Did I use jargon without explaining?

**Product Thinking** (Depth)
- Did I ask clarifying questions?
- Did I identify the root problem before jumping to solutions?
- Did I consider multiple approaches?

**Framework Usage** (Structure)
- Did I use a structured approach?
- Did I cover all key dimensions?
- Did I know when to switch frameworks?

**Metrics Literacy** (Numbers)
- Did I define success metrics early?
- Did I propose realistic metrics (not generic)?
- Did I explain why each metric matters?

**User Empathy** (Insight)
- Did I genuinely think about the user's life?
- Did I consider multiple user segments?
- Did I acknowledge constraints users face?

**Realism** (Feasibility)
- Was my solution achievable in timeframe?
- Did I acknowledge tradeoffs?
- Did I consider implementation challenges?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Starting with solutions** → Always understand the problem first
2. **Designing for everyone** → Pick a specific user segment
3. **Ignoring metrics** → Define success early and often
4. **Making up data** → Be clear about assumptions vs. facts
5. **Changing direction without explanation** → Always explain your pivot
6. **Talking too much** → Check in with interviewer: "Does this make sense?"
7. **Memorized answers** → Practice the thinking process, not the answer
8. **No clarifying questions** → Ask 3-5 questions at the start
9. **Ignoring business constraints** → "That's too expensive" is a real constraint
10. **Spending 30 min on one aspect** → Manage your time across all dimensions

## Final Thoughts

Product sense interviews are about **how you think**, not what you know. These 63+ questions are practice reps—not things to memorize.

The best preparation:
1. Learn the frameworks (3-4 hours)
2. Practice the thinking process (20-30 hours over 6-8 weeks)
3. Get feedback from peers (3-5 hours)
4. Do full mock interviews (4-6 hours)

Total prep: 30-45 hours for strong performance.

Focus on **process over perfection**. Interviewers expect you to think out loud, ask questions, and refine your approach. They're evaluating your thinking, not hunting for errors.

Good luck with your interviews!
