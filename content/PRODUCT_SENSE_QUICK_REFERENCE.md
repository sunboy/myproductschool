## Red Flags (Things That Will Hurt Your Score)

- Jumping to solutions before understanding the user
- Designing for "everyone" instead of picking a specific segment
- Making up metrics instead of reasoning about what matters
- Not acknowledging tradeoffs
- Changing your approach without explaining why
- Assuming the interviewer knows what you mean without explaining
- Spending 30 minutes on one aspect and rushing the rest
- Sounding like you memorized an answer instead of thinking through it
- Not asking clarifying questions
- Making claims without data or logic

---

## Green Flags (Things That Will Raise Your Score)

- Start by asking 3-5 clarifying questions
- Define target user explicitly and early
- Show your thinking process out loud
- Acknowledge constraints and tradeoffs upfront
- Reference real products and observations
- Include both qualitative user insights and quantitative metrics
- Change direction when interviewer provides new info
- Check in with interviewer: "Does this direction make sense?"
- Provide multiple options and explain why you chose one
- Wrap up with clear summary of your approach and rationale

## Time Allocation (45-minute interview)

- **0-5 min:** Introduction and question clarification
- **5-10 min:** Ask clarifying questions, define target user/scope
- **10-25 min:** Deep dive into problem and user research
- **25-35 min:** Propose solutions and features
- **35-40 min:** Metrics and success measurement
- **40-45 min:** Closing, summary, and interviewer questions

## Metrics Cheat Sheet

**Always consider these metric categories:**

**Acquisition**
- New user signups
- Download counts
- Cost per acquisition (CAC)
- Source of acquisition

**Engagement**
- Daily active users (DAU)
- Monthly active users (MAU)
- Session length
- Feature usage rate

**Retention**
- Day 1 retention
- Day 7 retention
- Day 30 retention
- Churn rate

**Monetization**
- Average revenue per user (ARPU)
- Lifetime value (LTV)
- Conversion rate
- Revenue per transaction

**Growth/Viral**
- Net promoter score (NPS)
- Referral rate
- Viral coefficient
- Time to invite friend

## Tradeoff Decision Framework

When faced with "A or B?" questions, structure your answer:

1. **Define the tradeoff** - Explain why you can't do both right now
2. **State what's at stake** - What do we gain/lose with each choice?
3. **Identify success metric** - Which metric matters most?
4. **Make the call** - Choose A or B with reasoning
5. **Explain sequence** - Do this first, then that
6. **Acknowledge risk** - What could go wrong?

## Frameworks Deep Dive

### CIRCLES Method (Product Design)
- **C**omprehend the situation
- **I**dentify users
- **R**ecognize user needs
- **C**ut through with priorities
- **L**ist solutions
- **E**valuate tradeoffs
- **S**ummarize recommendation

### GAME Framework (Any Question)
- **G**oals: What are we trying to achieve?
- **A**ction: What will we do?
- **M**etrics: How will we measure?
- **E**valuation: Was it successful?

### AARRR Framework (Growth/Metrics)
- **A**cquisition: How do users find us?
- **A**ctivation: How do they get engaged?
- **R**etention: How do we keep them?
- **R**evenue: How do we make money?
- **R**eferral: How do they bring others?

## Mental Models to Master

**Before Interview:**

1. **User Empathy** - Get visceral understanding of user's life
2. **Problem vs. Solution** - Confirm problem before jumping to solution
3. **Metrics First** - Know what success looks like before proposing
4. **Constraints** - Understand the real limitations early
5. **Competitive Context** - Know what competitors are doing
6. **Network Effects** - How does each user action create value for others?
7. **Unit Economics** - Can the business sustain this approach?
8. **Iteration** - It's not about the first idea, it's the thinking process

## Practice Schedule (8-Week Prep)

**Week 1-2: Fundamentals**
- Learn CIRCLES, GAME, AARRR frameworks
- Practice 2 simple design questions

**Week 3-4: Breadth**
- Do 2 questions per category
- Record yourself, listen back
- Have peer review your thinking

**Week 5-6: Company-Specific**
- Practice 3 questions from your target company
- Study their product deeply (use it for a week)
- Know their metrics and recent product moves

**Week 7-8: Mock Interviews**
- Do 2-3 full mock interviews with peers
- Simulate real time pressure
- Get feedback on clarity and structure
