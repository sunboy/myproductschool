Compare the model's output to the expected output, compute a metric, track the metric over time. This works well for classification, extraction, and other tasks where the answer space is constrained.\n\nFor generation tasks, ground truth evals are often incomplete. A customer support agent that writes email responses might produce an answer that is factually correct, professionally worded, and the right length, but also accidentally includes the customer's account number in the response body. The ground-truth eval would score it highly. Users and compliance teams would not.\n\nBehavioral contracts fill this gap. A behavioral contract is a property that the output must satisfy, regardless of what the specific content is. It does not require comparing to a reference answer. It requires checking that certain conditions hold.\n\n## Defining a Behavioral Contract\n\nA behavioral contract is a predicate over the outputs of a system.
