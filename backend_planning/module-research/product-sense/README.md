# Product Sense Module — Research Briefs

Module slug: `product-sense`
Difficulty: `entry-point`
Chapters: 7
Target reader: engineers (SWE, tech lead, staff, founding, EM) who want to be evaluated as product thinkers without first becoming PMs.

## Arc

The module argues a single thesis across seven chapters: **product sense is a named, trainable skill, not a gift.** Engineers tend to arrive with most of the raw material already but lack the vocabulary and the reps. Each chapter names one piece of the vocabulary and points to a concrete way to practice it.

- **Ch 1** opens the argument: engineers have intuition, just not the words for it.
- **Ch 2** names the mindset shift from "how" to "why" as the single biggest unlock.
- **Ch 3** maps Gergely Orosz's nine traits of product-minded engineers onto daily work.
- **Ch 4** introduces the Why-First Check as a three-part gate for any feature idea.
- **Ch 5** catalogues the four failure modes interviewers see most, so the reader can spot them in their own thinking.
- **Ch 6** attacks the framework-recitation trap that PM interview prep culture has created.
- **Ch 7** gives a concrete weekly rep schedule engineers can run in their current job.
