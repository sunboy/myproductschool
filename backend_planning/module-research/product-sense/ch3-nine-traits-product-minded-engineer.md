# Ch 3: The nine traits of a product-minded engineer

## Hook

Gergely Orosz's nine traits are not a checklist for a personality test. They are nine specific habits that, together, describe the engineer every PM wishes they had on every team.

## Core idea

Orosz wrote the canonical list in 2019 after years at Uber, Microsoft, Skype, and Skyscanner. Each trait is a small behavior the engineer can practice independently of permission from a PM, manager, or career ladder. The chapter walks through the nine traits, names the engineering-native version of each, and points at which ones the reader probably already has versus which ones take deliberate reps.

## Quotes

- Orosz, G. *The Product-Minded Software Engineer*, 2019: "Product-minded engineers don't settle for getting a specification and jumping to implement it. They think about other ideas and approach the product manager with these. They often challenge existing specifications, suggesting alternative product approaches."
- Orosz, same essay: "Through repeated cycles of questioning, suggesting improvements, building, and learning from real-world results, their product judgment strengthens considerably over time."
- Sherif Mansour (PM, Atlassian), cited in Orosz's essay: "product engineers are a critical ingredient to helping you build a successful product" and they scale product management effectiveness.

## The nine traits (verbatim list from Orosz)

1. Proactive with product ideas and opinions
2. Interest in business, user behavior, and data
3. Curiosity and keen interest in "why?"
4. Strong communicators with non-engineers
5. Offering product/engineering tradeoffs upfront
6. Pragmatic handling of edge cases
7. Quick product validation cycles
8. End-to-end product feature ownership
9. Strong product instincts built through repeated cycles

## Named examples

- **Jean-Michel Lemieux, then Head of Engineering at Shopify**: described by Orosz as someone who embodies trait 3 (keen "why") and trait 8 (end-to-end ownership). Lemieux's quote in the same essay: product engineers are those who engage with the "why" actively and understand that "minimum lovable products need the right depth" during building.
- **Ebi Atawodi, Director of PM at YouTube Studio, formerly at Uber and Netflix**: cited by Orosz on traits of standout engineers on X/Twitter in 2022. Source: <https://x.com/GergelyOrosz/status/1917957512462057857>.

## Engineering analog

Most of the nine traits have direct engineering mirrors: trait 6 (pragmatic edge cases) is what a good SRE does every week, trait 7 (quick validation) is canary deploys, trait 8 (end-to-end ownership) is the DevOps principle "you build it, you run it". The two that tend to need new reps are trait 1 (proactive with product ideas) and trait 3 (keen "why"), because the engineer was explicitly told in most cultures that those are not their job.

## Anti-pattern to catch

The reader treating the nine traits as a checklist to pass rather than as a vocabulary for observations they can make starting Monday. The chapter should explicitly reject "do all nine on day one" and instead point at picking one trait to practice for a week.

## Suggested diagram

A 3x3 grid of nine cards, each with the trait name on top and a one-line engineering analog below. Two cards should be visually highlighted (primary green border) as the "need new reps" cluster for engineers specifically: trait 1 (proactive ideas) and trait 3 (keen "why").
